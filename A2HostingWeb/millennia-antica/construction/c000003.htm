Right now, this page is a place-holder for what will be described.  There is already a working template for construction-structure pages though.

For the prototype work, this material need not be complete.  It is offered as representative of how a self-documenting site is prepared to leave a development site in place that others can pick up, review and extend.  It is also, in the case study, a basis for an integration site that may be incremented as different production stages are reached.  It provides a reference site in that regard, for comparison of prototype and production elements, and for having a test harness for production elements.

  • This page describes the construction structure of the site, including how index.html and construction.htm pages are used in every section.   

  • The nature of the construction block at the bottom of every page is explained.

  • The nature of the grounding and version block in the upper right-hand corner is explained

  • The way the construction structures are linked together and to the construction section of a mirror is explained

  • Minimal accommodation of cloaking in the construction section is also specified.

  • For the construction section itself, and structures superior to the mirrored site, there will be separate explanations. 

Every section (mini-Web) of the Millennia Antica site has an index.htm page.  These pages accomplish three things:

  • ensure that the index of every Millennia Antica directory is hidden and the only access to material of the site is via navigation or use of a known, published URL
  • provide a default page for entry to areas when a default.htm (or default.asp ) page is missing during site construction and updates.
  • tie together the construction structure for the entire Millennia Antica site.

For example, if you reach this page by navigating directly to Millennia Antica /construction , you know that this section of the web site is itself under construction and any default.htm page is missing (or non-existent).  You'll have to return later to see if this section of the site ever provides anything more than its own Hard Hat area.
