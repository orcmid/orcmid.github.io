2002-10-20-06:33 Rename Items to Match Up with Activity Log (orcmid)
I am migrating to an accession-oriented numbering system for the pages.  For example, the 1983 Big Creek Pottery page is now A830600-BigCreekPottery.htm.  This allows a more-organized approach to naming of material with all pages having catalog identifications.  Some will also have meaningful names where required.  I am then freed from having to make up unique and memorable names all of the time, even for miniscule items.
  1. I started on the assumption that the site is sufficiently new that breaking any external bookmarks to individual activity pages is not a great risk.  But my promise is that the way the nfoCentrale.net sites are maintained, no possible links to navigation-reachable pages would ever be broken.  Since I can't be assured that there are no bookmarks, I should be making tombstones.  So I will:
  2. This is better documented as part of the web construction and maintenance for Centrale.  I will handle that in the future.  Right now I want a better organization right here so that I can have the revised organizations to assist in the introduction and maintenance of more activity material.
  3. Before a page is renamed on the development site, it is "branched" in the configuration management system.  Then the page is renamed on the development site and the newly-named version is shared back to the upload image too.  The older version, under the original name, is still present in the configuration-managed upload image.  That one is shared back to the development site.  Then the older version is edited into a tombstone.  At some point, when I expect that I will never touch the tombstone again, I can remove it from the development image only, leaving it in the upload image.  I don't have to resolve that right now.
