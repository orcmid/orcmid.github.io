1. Choosing Personas

1.1 Alan Cooper enjoins us to design products for just one single person (1999, p.124).  In the present setting, that would be me and the result would be decidedly uninteresting.  The way Cooper, in the interaction design methodology, avoids making products that have a vague rubbery and over-abstracted user assumption or one so specific that only the author fits, is to invent hypothetical personas.  These personas, as few in number as possible, are hypothetical yet representative characterizations.  They nail down enough points of the "user space" to provide grounding and reality testing for the product.

1.2 In this case, the product is not a single thing.  It is a couple of places on the Internet populated by documents and other digital artifacts, including programs.  It is a series of activities and projects in which others may become involved.  It is a spiraling of learning and improvement in which many people, mostly unknown to the producers and each other, may be engaged.

1.3 There are currently two personas that represent the ODMA community and those engaged in whatever comes out of the ActiveODMA activity.  They don't seem to be terribly pre-occupied by that momentous responsibility.  It would seem that they are simply having way too much fun.

2. Urbi Fareed

2.1 Urbi Fareed is the daughter of a Pakistani petroleum engineer and an Egyptian forensic pathologist specializing in the analysis of ancient burials and tombs.  Her parents met when a pipeline crew disturbed an ancient tomb.

2.2 Urbi is completing a course of Computer Studies at the Higher Technical Institute in Nicosia, Cyprus.  She works for a small firm that wishes to introduce a locally-developed document and image-management product into Southern Europe and Northern Africa.  Ms. Fareed is charged with determining how to make the product ODMA-aware as a way to simplify integration and establish confidence in the product's interoperability with office software.  A key challenge is providing for the several languages used in the market area.

2.3 Urbi is a bright student and a methodical worker.  She feels awkward with technical English and is not always sure that she understands the detailed materials that she must deal with.

3. Miguel Delgado

3.1 Miguel (Mike) Delgado is the Director of Information Systems and Operations for a county court and records system in the the State of New Mexico.  Miguel received his first training in computer systems while a seaman in the United States Navy.  He entered civil employment on leaving military service.  He since completed Associates and Bachelors degrees in computer technology and business (MIS).

3.2 Miguel's organization is facing its second major conversion effort.  Conversions from midrange systems to client-server systems was completed over five years ago.  There is now an effort to consolidate isolated document management systems onto a set of consistent platforms for uniform preservation of public records and court documents.  With the streamlined use of digital documents in all departments, the current use of ODMA for integration is being looked at as a way to move to newer technology without interrupting existing practices.  Desktop systems are being gradually replaced with current products and software.

3.3 Although Microsoft Office products are the principle tools in departmental work, official records are converted to Adobe PDF for retention and HTML for public access as part of the State's eGovernment mandate.   Some custom systems are used in the court system and the criminal justice offices; they are isolated from routine activities of the civil government departments. 

3.4 There is discussion of using XML formats and emerging standards for document formats.  Miguel, a veteran of the previous conversion, is concerned about preservation of legacy materials and operating mixed systems until all of the technology is upgraded and stable.  Based on experiences upgrading e-mail systems in the past, Miguel is also concerned with trouble-shooting and staff demands for user support during the changeover period.
