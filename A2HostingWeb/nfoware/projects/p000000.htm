  2004-01-18 I have been talking, in notes, about spiraling a lot.  ("Talking in Notes."  I must remember that.)  It looks like there is actually a project to define what I mean by spiraling, capture spirals, and also make clear what I mean by it.  I am thinking in spiral development of nfoWare (the site for starters).  The key case for spiraling is the chicken-and-egg problem around confirmable experience and coherence.   I need a fair amount of material for this to be demonstrated with the site itself and the artifacts provided as nfoWare.   Spiraling is, in this context, a way to make one "round" of development, knowing full well that it alludes to more than is provided, yet somehow start closing on things.  One wrap of the spiral is one layer of expansion and, in an informal sense, completiing/closing.  It is not the same as incremental, because rework and replacement may be involved.  However, here we will retain residue of all previous spirals so that we have a demonstration of how it was done (and how it wasn't).  I really like incremental development and evolutionary development, so we will see how that and a spiral development model fit together. (I might also be misapplying these terms very severely, so I need to check that out to.)  Something to define in a project so this can be undertaken and shown in a self-conscious way.
