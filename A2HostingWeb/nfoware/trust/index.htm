You're at the /trust section of an nfoWare.com web site.  The early work in this section deals with trust in the context of  Situating Data.  The initial materials may be rather disorganized but this provides an initial place for collecting what can be refined later.

Trust is a very broad context, but some of the trust-related technologies come up fairly early in the treatment of data:

  • digital signatures, Public-Key Infrastructure (PKI), its use of data and its application to data (including computer programs in their various forms).
  • The trust structures created around the software tools, utilities, and other artifacts that are nfoWare.
  • The confirmable experience with regard to trust and PKI and the digital signing of data and transactions.
  • The notion of digital identity and all that entails in the joining of the world of human action and digital communication and coordination.

For the moment, this is not an all-encompassing treatment of trust, reliance of formal methods, and so on.  For broader treatment of trust, consult

  • The Orcmid's Lair bibliography on Trust and Trustworthy Computing
  • Any NuovoDoc treatment of Trust as an architectural issue
  • Toolcraft-related discussion of Trust and trust technologies (perhaps here).
  • The TROST Project and its web site.

A major challenge for treatment of trust here is that we are looking, primarily at the situation of data related to trust.  There will be at least one Trust Spiral round on creating trust structures that apply to nfoWare.  It is not clear how that will be handled just yet, and what further rounds will be needed beyond the first.
