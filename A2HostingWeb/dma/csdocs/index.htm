You've reached www.infonuovo.com/dma/csdocs, a collection of information about the Compound-Structured Documents Extensions to the DMA 1.0 Specification:

  • what is CSDocs,
  • how it fits with DMA 1.0,
  • how CSDocs is used to provide interoperability among compound-structured documents systems,
  • the benefits of CSDocs,
  • how CSDocs fits in the overall scheme of things and considerations for introduction in an interoperable way with other standards and other DMA implementations
  • sources of more information

This and other sections of infonuovo.com/dma provide an unofficial guide to the DMA Technical Committee effort on the DMA 1.0 specification, proposed extensions, and trial-use recommendations.  Other InfoNuovo sites cover work separate from that of the DMA Technical Committee:

  • DMware, an unofficial guide to the open-source follow-on to DMA 1.0, ODMA 2.0, and other document-management interoperability projects.
  • NuovoDoc Centrale, other explorations of the New Electronic Document and collaborative projects conducted over the web.

This web page, www.infonuovo.com/dma/csdocs/index.htm, is part of the engineering, construction management, maintenance, and infrastructure of the DMA site.  You are welcome to explore this and other construction-information pages.   You'll need your hard hat and safety shoes.

If you'd rather find out about DMA's approach to Compound-Structured Documents than admire the scaffolding, organization, and implementation of the infonuovo.com site, go directly to the DMA/CSDocs web folder.   If that doesn't work, come back later.  The folder is blocked off whenever the construction crew is working here.
