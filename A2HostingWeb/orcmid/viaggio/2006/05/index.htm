This folder provides Viaggio photographic images from roll F0605, begun and completed on February 10, 2006, and processed on February 14:

  1. 2006-02-10: Continuing at Moose Camp: More people in a room.   Not sure what session it is.
  2. Ponzi and Chris in the back of a room.
  3. More on-lookers, interesting for a montage.
  4. Mark Hamilton presents on Citizen Journalism.  This got Chris agitated at the end, causing a discussion about what makes journalists "professional" that Mark had hoped for at the beginning.  We didn't get to embark into this topic which is unfortunate, because Mark is an instructor of journalism and a long-time professional.  On a panel the next day, one of the journalist panelists, a long-time pro, pointed out that all he could see that made him a journalist was that he was paid to do it.
  5. Audience.
  6. Session on Structured Content.  Guy in audience working at his laptop.
  7. Robert Scoble in the front of the room, doing the usual thing.
  8. Woman making a comment (not sure which session this is).
  9. Robert turns to talk to the fellow behind him.
  10. Bryan Rieger presenting on structured content.  There's more of what Bryan's up to on Yiibu.
  11. An audience member that I saw in many of my wanderings.  Apparently a working journalist.  I don't have a name.
  12. Boris Lamb.  This is a great profile shot.
  13. Chatting after Bryan's discussion.
  14. Julie Leung chatting with someone
  15. Ted Leung is taking my picture taking his picture.
  16. Anita Roland showing off her knitting while listening to the lunch-time chat.
  17. Ted Leung takes my picture with a different configuration of his rig.
  18. Talking over lunch, with John Hartman speaking across the table.
  19. Blogging and the Future of Media. This presentation provided an analysis of gateways and channels and how this is can be expected to change.  A number of disruptions are pointed out.
  20. Blogging in Community session, with Nancy White.  Nancy has blogged about this session, keeping notes like crazy.
  21. One of the particpants speaks up.
  22. The press guy says something that has me think about some stuff.
  23. More participants.
  24. Phillip Jeffrey reports on why tagging works.  I also looked at the Lenova X41 that he was using to make his presentation.  I was disappointed that it has no touch pad.
