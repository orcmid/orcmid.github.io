I am looking forward to attending your session at AIIM 99.

I wanted to catch up with you on a number of related activities that I think you are  a powerful evangelist for, and get your thinking.

1.      Thanks for the discussions with Richard Sauvain on requirements for Compound/Structured Documents.  We still haven't stepped back to requirements around what matters to users of DMA-compliant products, but you gave us something to think about.
        [When we were polishing up DMA 1.0 we never did go back to the requirements that we developed earlier in the work.  That all disappeared except for the high-level bullet requirements that don't say much about measurable satisfaction.]
        I am interested in deepening the relationship of user requirements and the technical responses in DMA 1.0, its extensions, and any DMAware that is developed to make DMA-compliant products easier to use.
        I am learning a lot about making requirements measurable and related to something that matters to people!
        How is the Black Forest Group work on requirements continuing or progressing?  I would like to see cleaner requirements and a recognizable relationship between requirements and the reference model work.
        What are your thoughts?

2.      I am focusing on Integration for Interoperability.  That is and has been a theme for me in my participation in DMA, and it still calls to me.  But we have done this at a technical level and not addressed very powerfully what integration for interoperability provides for end-user organizations and enterprise information technology.  I see a gap to cover between what we have at the DMA 1.0 API level and what people need to have in order to integrate and deploy at the application and business function level.
        I want to explore this gap and ways to fill it that gives our enterprise user community something to sink their teeth into.  I think it would also identify opportunities to provide value that supports enterprise integration for interoperability.
        That's what I have been thinking about.  Just how to say this much.
        How does this land with you?  Does this seem fruitful?

Regards,

        Dennis
